What makes it different

The fruit isn't a flavor. It's the vodka.

Most flavored vodkas start with a base and add syrup. Sukari infuses whole fruit and vegetables into the spirit itself, so what you taste is what's in the bottle. Nothing artificial goes in after.

01 · Infused, not flavored

You can see what's in it

Real blood orange, dragonfruit, pineapple and pear are infused in. Beets give the natural rosy color. Hold it to the light and you see the produce, not a dye.

02 · The name

Sweet, with nothing added

"Sukari" means sugar in Swahili and Arabic, a nod to how naturally sweet it tastes. No added sugar, no syrup, no sweetener. The sweetness is all from the fruit.

03 · How it drinks

No burn, and you can sip it neat

It goes down smooth enough to drink straight, tastes like a fruity drink, and people consistently report no hangover. One of the cleaner spirits you can pour.

Her story

How Ro built Sukari

Sukari was built by Rolesha "Ro" Brown, a service-disabled veteran and single mother who started with a recipe and her word. A few of the moments that got the bottle onto the shelf.

Servicebefore the bottle

A veteran's discipline

Ro served first. The same patience and follow-through carried into building a spirit from nothing, as a single mother, with no shortcuts.

The recipea first of its kind

The first fruit-and-vegetable infused vodka

No one had infused whole produce into a premium vodka this way. Ro did the work to make it taste like fruit while staying true to what vodka is, with no sugar added.

What you see in it is the fruits and vegetables. Rolesha Brown, founder
2022recognition

Judged, and awarded

Bartender Spirits Awards Silver. San Diego Spirits Festival Bronze for the vodka, and a separate award for the bottle design. The curve and cutout of the bottle were drawn to represent a woman's curves.

Nowpaying it forward

Building room for others

The next chapter is a bottling effort meant to help other independent and Black-owned brands get made, the kind of help Ro didn't have starting out. The phoenix on the bottle is the point: built from the ground up, rising.

Pour it

Good neat. Better shared.

It's smooth enough to sip on its own, and it carries a cocktail without a single drop of syrup. A few ways people pour it.

Tall & easy

Tropical Bay Breeze

3 oz Sukari over ice, 2 oz pineapple, an orange wheel.

Bright & short

Blood Orange Gimlet

3 oz Sukari, a good squeeze of fresh lime, an orange wedge.

Classic

Sukari Mule

3 oz Sukari, 4 oz ginger beer, lime over ice in a copper mug.

Porch pour

Spiked Peach Sweet Tea

3 oz Sukari, 4 oz brewed tea, fresh peach. No sugar needed.

Long & cold

Garnet Spritz

3 oz Sukari, 2 oz soda, a squeeze of grapefruit over plenty of ice.

Up & elegant

Phoenix Sour

3 oz Sukari, ¾ oz lemon, a dash of bitters, shaken and served up.

Tip Try it straight first. That's the part most people don't believe until they do.
